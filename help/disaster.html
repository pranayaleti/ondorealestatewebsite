Common Utah Disasters

Earthquakes
Utah is prone to earthquakes. Know what to do before, during, and after an earthquake.

Immediate Actions:

  • • Drop, cover, and hold on
  • • Stay indoors if safe
  • • Avoid doorways and windows

After the Event:

  • • Check for injuries
  • • Inspect property damage
  • • Contact insurance
  • • Document damage
Floods
Flash floods and river flooding can cause significant property damage in Utah.

Immediate Actions:

  • • Move to higher ground
  • • Avoid driving through floodwaters
  • • Turn off utilities if safe

After the Event:

  • • Document damage with photos
  • • Contact insurance immediately
  • • Avoid contaminated areas
  • • Get professional cleanup
Wildfires
Wildfire season brings smoke, evacuation orders, and potential property damage.

Immediate Actions:

  • • Follow evacuation orders
  • • Close all windows and doors
  • • Turn off air conditioning

After the Event:

  • • Wait for all-clear before returning
  • • Check air quality
  • • Inspect property for damage
  • • Contact insurance
Severe Weather
Hail, wind, and severe storms can cause significant damage to homes and property.

Immediate Actions:

  • • Seek shelter indoors
  • • Stay away from windows
  • • Avoid electrical equipment

After the Event:

  • • Check for damage
  • • Take photos of any damage
  • • Contact insurance
  • • Make temporary repairs if safe

Mortgage Assistance During Disasters

Forbearance
Temporarily pause mortgage payments during disaster recovery.

Duration:

Up to 12 months

Requirements:

Documented disaster impact

Payment Deferral
Add missed payments to the end of your loan term.

Duration:

Varies by situation

Requirements:

Temporary hardship due to disaster

Loan Modification
Permanently adjust loan terms to make payments more affordable.

Duration:

Permanent change

Requirements:

Long-term financial impact

Disaster Preparedness Tips

  • • Create an emergency kit with water, food, and supplies for 72 hours
  • • Keep important documents in a waterproof, portable container
  • • Have a family communication plan and meeting place
  • • Know your insurance coverage and keep policy numbers handy
  • • Take photos of your property and belongings for insurance purposes
  • • Sign up for emergency alerts from your local government
